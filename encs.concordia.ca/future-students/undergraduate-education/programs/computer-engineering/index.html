Student Academic Services Office

Student Academic Services (SAS) is the office that deals with admissions and advising. SAS is the place to go to: obtain general advising; receive general information about ENCS Departments; inquire about the admission requirements for programs offered by ENCS; find out what is needed to change programs; access information on transfer credits, student exchanges, visiting students and student requests; and apply for readmission.

If you need additional information please do not hesitate to contact SAS by phoning +1-514-848-2424, ext. 3055 or 3057.



As a computer engineer, what would I do?

Computer engineers design, build, and test computing systems for general purpose use, personal computers for example, as well as for dedicated controllers built into devices like MP3 readers, DVD players and recorders, or even today's washing machines. Computer engineers have the hardware and software skills and knowledge to understand, develop, and integrate these ubiquitous systems.

Why Choose Computer Engineering at Concordia?

Concordia's Computer Engineering program provides a solid grounding in engineering mathematics and science, programming methodology, and digital electronics. A system hardware engineer might design the hardware module that controls the fuel injection system in a car, with sensors that determine the position of the crankshaft, the speed of rotation, and the position of the accelerator pedal in order to send the right quantity of fuel to the cylinders. A system software engineer might write the program that runs on the hardware module, and that determines how much fuel to inject into each cylinder and when, based on the power required by the driver and the car's speed. Such real-time embedded systems are a feature component of our program, with its extensive hands-on design and programming labs. The emphasis is on practical work, with state-of-the-art labs in some 30 courses.

Our program offers small class sizes. Our faculty members are established researchers who bring their cutting-edge knowledge and experience to the classroom to enrich the student learning experience.

In the final year of the program, students team up on a special project to design a solution to a significant engineering problem and implement their design as a working device or prototype.

Our program is accredited by the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board and our graduates are eligible to become registered professional engineers.

Computer Engineering options:

  • System Hardware – develop expertise in digital electronics, digital system design, and VLSI circuits for the design of specialized hardware for real time embedded systems.
  • System Software - develop expertise in real-time system software, software testing and validation, communication networks, and software design for applications in real time embedded systems.
