Student Academic Services Office

Student Academic Services (SAS) is the office that deals with admissions and advising. SAS is the place to go to: obtain general advising; receive general information about ENCS Departments; inquire about the admission requirements for programs offered by ENCS; find out what is needed to change programs; access information on transfer credits, student exchanges, visiting students and student requests; and apply for readmission.

If you need additional information please do not hesitate to contact SAS by phoning +1-514-848-2424, ext. 3055 or 3057.

Information Session for Newly Admitted Students to the Engineering and Computer Science Faculty

January 2012 & Fall 2012

Welcome to the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science. In order to assist you we have put together this guideline.

1.  All students who have been admitted to either of our Engineering or Computer Science programs and have received a letter of admission are required to register for their courses on the web via the Student Portal. Review the “Registration Guide”, available at http://www.encs.concordia.ca/documents/ENCS-Registration-Guide.pdf . Please note that you are responsible for your registration.

2.  Confirm your attendance to one of the Faculty Information and Advising Sessions listed below by calling the Student Academic Services, at 514-848-2424 ext 3055, OR by reviewing the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science Information and Advising Guide for Newly Admitted Students, available at, http://www.encs.concordia.ca/scs/current-undergraduate.html (choose new students, then Advising Guide).

If you proceed with the on-line version of the Information and Advising Guide for Newly Admitted Students, you are not obliged to attend the orientation session in person BUT this would be a great opportunity for you to meet with faculty and staff, ask questions and meet other new students in your program.

Students from out-of-town, who are unable to attend a session in person or are having problems accessing the on-line version of the Advising Guide, should contact Ms. Sonia McCalla, at 514-848-2424 ext 3055 (sonia@encs.concordia.ca).

For information concerning your program of study, please contact your department representative(s) at the following:

3. Review the ‘Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science Information and Advising Guide for Newly Admitted Students Fall 2012’ which is available on the internet at : http://www.encs.concordia.ca/scs under New Students - Advising Guide.

4. Read over all the information that was provided to you in your admission package by the Office of the Registrar.  Follow-up on any outstanding issues.

Newly admitted students with post-secondary studies (e.g. previous university studies) or Concordia degree transfer students should review the request for Advanced Standing and Exemptions Form included in your acceptance package.  Should this apply to you, please ensure that you submit the completed form to the Faculty as soon as possible.

5.  Review the Undergraduate Calendar and the Undergraduate Class Schedule available online at www.concordia.ca.

6. Bring your letter of admission and other documents to the Information and Advising Session, so that we can assist you with any question you may have.
