Jobs

 

Staff positions

If you are interested in becoming an ENCS staff member, be sure to check the job postings on Concordia's Human Resources website.



Academic positions currently available:

  • Building, Civil and Environmental Engineering (3 tenure-track positions)
  • Computer Science and Software Engineering (1 tenure-track position)
  • Electrical and Computer Engineering (1 tenure-track position)
  • Mechanical and Industrial Engineering (2 tenure-track positions)
  • Limited Term Appointment in Social and Ethical Dimensions of Engineering (1 position)

Applications are invited for the following full-time, tenure-track faculty positions at the assistant or associate professor level. Applicants will be expected to demonstrate a strong commitment to supervision of MASc and PhD students, and to excellence in teaching at both the graduate and undergraduate levels.  They will conduct independent scholarly research and attract strong external funding.

Applications should consist of detailed curriculum vitae, a statement concerning teaching and research interests and the names of at least three referees.

Relevant industrial experience is an asset. Excellent communication skills are required.  Knowledge of French is an asset but not essential for an academic career at Concordia. Membership or eligibility for membership in a Canadian professional engineering association, preferably in the province of Quebec, is required.

Review of the applications will begin immediately and will continue until the positions are filled.  Electronic applications are preferred and should be sent to the appropriate department contact by email no later than November 1, 2011. Only short-listed applicants will be notified.

Subject to budgetary approval, we anticipate filling these positions, normally at the rank of Assistant Professor, for July 1, 2012. Appointments at a more senior level may also be considered for some of the positions advertised. Unless otherwise stipulated in the descriptions on our website, candidates for all positions should have a PhD. Review of applications will begin immediately and will continue until the positions are filled.

All inquiries about specific positions should be directed to the appropriate departmental contact. All qualified candidates are encouraged to apply; however, Canadian citizens and permanent residents of Canada will be given priority. Concordia University is committed to employment equity.

BUILDING, CIVIL AND ENVIRONMENTAL ENGINEERING
Dr. M. Zaheeruddin - Chair, BCEE
zaheer@bcee.concordia.ca |  www.bcee.concordia.ca
We invite applications for positions in the following areas:

Environmental Engineering – Applications are sought in the general area of environmental engineering with expertise and interest in one or more of the following areas: industrial waste treatment, carbon capture, net-zero discharge systems, environmental systems engineering, environmental forensics and disaster management. Applicants must hold a PhD in Environmental Engineering or related disciplines.

Building Engineering – Applications are sought in the general area of building engineering with expertise and interest in one or more of the following areas: building acoustics, lighting/daylighting, sustainable built environment, artificial intelligence applications in building systems, integrated systems and energy efficiency. Applicants must hold a PhD in Building or Architectural engineering, or related disciplines. 

Building Engineering – Applicants must have a PhD in building or architectural engineering, or related disciplines and must have a track record in the building envelope analysis and design with expertise in one or more of the following areas: Heat, Air and Moisture (HAM) building performance, modeling, building diagnostic and failure analysis, condition assessment, and rehabilitation of building envelopes. Expertise in experimental testing and practical design experience is desirable.

COMPUTER SCIENCE AND SOFTWARE ENGINEERING
Dr. S. P. Mudur - Chair, CSE
chair@cse.concordia.ca | www.cse.concordia.ca
We invite applications for a position in the following area:

Software Engineering – Applicants must demonstrate specific expertise and research interests in software architecture and design. Applicants must hold a PhD in Software Engineering, Computer Science or Computer Engineering.

ELECTRICAL AND COMPUTER ENGINEERING
Dr. W.E. Lynch - Chair, ECE
blynch@ece.concordia.ca | www.ece.concordia.ca
We invite applications for a position in the following area:

Computer Engineering (Software) – Applicants must possess solid expertise and research interests in cloud computing, virtualization, and quality of service engineering and monitoring.  Knowledge of software engineering techniques is required. Applicants must hold a PhD in Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Computer Science or Software Engineering.

MECHANICAL AND INDUSTRIAL ENGINEERING
Dr. M. D. Pugh - Chair, MIE
chair@mie.concordia.ca | www.mie.concordia.ca
We invite applications for positions in the following two areas:

Aerospace Engineering – Applications are sought specifically in the area of aircraft structures, stress analysis and aero-elasticity. Applicants should hold a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical/Aeronautical Engineering or similar engineering disciplines with a PhD in Mechanical/Aeronautical Engineering or in a closely related area.

Composites Manufacturing – The candidate is expected to fill a junior research chair position, complementing the research activities of a senior NSERC Industrial chair in Automated Composites Manufacturing. Applicants should hold a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering or similar engineering disciplines, with a PhD degree in Mechanical or Composite Engineering or in a closely related area. Applicants with expertise in the area of Advanced Manufacturing of Polymer Matrix Composites and Composites for Aerospace Applications will be given preference.

LTA Position in Social and Ethical Dimensions of Engineering

The Centre for Engineering in Society (CES) of the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science, Concordia University, Montreal, invites applications for a Limited Term Appointment (LTA). This is a full-time position for 17 months beginning January 2012 and ending May 31, 2013.The CES teaches courses in the social implications of technology from a social science and humanities perspective; the successful candidate will teach undergraduate courses in social and ethical dimensions of engineering and technology, and in critical thinking.

Required Qualifications: Preference will be given to candidates holding a PhD in history, sociology or philosophy of science and technology, or another discipline that will contribute to CES’s mission. A demonstrated ability in teaching, preferably with large classes, is an asset.  The appointment will be at the rank of Lecturer or Assistant Professor, dependent upon the applicant’s degree and qualifications.

All qualified candidates are encouraged to apply; however, Canadians and Permanent Residents will be given priority. Concordia University is strongly committed to employment equity within its community, and to recruiting a diverse faculty and staff. The University encourages applications from all qualified candidates, including women, members of visible minorities, Aboriginal persons, members of sexual minorities, and persons with disabilities.

Consideration of applications will begin on November 18, 2011 and continue until the position is filled.

Submission will consist of: (i) a letter of application, (ii) a current curriculum vitae, (iii) a statement of the applicant’s understanding of the contributions of humanities and social science studies in engineering pedagogy, (iv) a statement of the applicant’s philosophy of teaching, and (v) evidence of teaching effectiveness. The complete dossier will contain three letters of reference sent directly from the candidate’s referees to Dr. Deborah Dysart-Gale.

Applications and inquiries should be submitted electronically to:
Dr. Deborah Dysart-Gale
Associate Professor and Chair
Centre for Engineering in Society
Engineering and Computer Science
Concordia University
Dysart@encs.concordia.ca
