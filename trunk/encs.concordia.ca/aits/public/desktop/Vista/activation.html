Windows Vista or Windows 7 Licensing and Activation

Changes in Licensing, and Activation

With the release of Windows Vista, Microsoft introduced two new types of Volume License Keys:
  • Multiple Activation Keys (MAK)
  • Key Management Service (KMS), and
AITS will only support KMS licensing

Multiple Activation Keys (MAK)
  • A MAK key is a 25 character serial number
  • MAK licenses the individual computer by connecting directly to the Microsoft servers
  • Re-imaging (i.e., cloning or rebuilding) a computer will require the use of a new MAK license

Key Management Service (KMS)
  • KMS licensing allows computers to connect to our own KMS server to activate
  • A computer that has been activated using KMS must be reactivated to the KMS server at least every six months
  • A KMS licensed computer that does not reactivate at least once every six (6) months, will automatically switch to a "reduced functionality mode" until reactivated
  • KMS activation is restricted to the ENCS network (which means, you need to be on our network to get activation).

How to Activate Windows Vista or Windows 7

To activate your Windows computer, you must have Administrator priviledged account, and you must connect to KMS server by:

a) having your computer reside on the ENCS network, or
b) by creating an ssh tunnel from your computer to the ENCS network, or
c) by connecting to the ENCS network via VPN (Note, that this service does not exist yet)

The AITS copies of the Windows Vista or Windows 7 media does not prompt you for a license key during installation. However, you must activate your copy within 30 days, or else your computer will automatically switch to a "reduced functionality mode" until reactivated.

To manually activate a computer with KMS:

  1. Open a command prompt as an administrator by either using the "runas" command, or by right clicking on  C:\Windows\System32\cmd.exe and selecting "Run as Administrator"
  2. Enter the command:
cscript c:\windows\system32\slmgr.vbs -skms kms.encs.concordia.ca:1688

then

cscript c:\windows\system32\slmgr.vbs -ato


NOTE: If you are trying to activate your copy via an ssh tunnel, after you have established a tunnel to and from port 1688, you need to set your KMS host to localhost:1688 in the above steps (There have been reports that using localhost (i.e., 127.0.0.1 may not work on Vista or Windows 7, as a workaround, use port 127.0.0.2).

REMEMBER: You need to allow your computer to "re-activate" itself at least once ever 180 days. Windows should do this automatically if you re-establish the ssh tunnel you created earlier. If not, simply redo the above steps.

To verify your activation status, check the system properties for the computer:
  1. Click on "Start"
  2. Click on "Control Panel"
  3. Click on "System and Maintenance"
  4. Click on "System"
  5. View information under Windows activation section
